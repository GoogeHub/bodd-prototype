We're building something genuinely meaningful. We want good humans to be part of it.

Bodd is an enterprise body intelligence platform founded in Melbourne and deployed globally — trusted by defence forces, global airlines, pharmaceutical companies, and health providers worldwide. We're building the infrastructure layer for human body data, and we're just getting started.

Get in Touch Shareholder Portal →

What we're building.

Every large organisation has people. And every one of those people has a body that changes over time — in ways that matter enormously to the organisations responsible for equipping them, caring for them, and keeping them performing at their best.

Until now, capturing that data has been slow, fragmented, expensive, and inconsistent. Different devices for different measurements. Specialist operators. Multiple appointments. No ongoing record. No population intelligence.

Bodd changes all of that. A single, self-serve scan captures a complete picture of the human body — from precise sizing and 3D modelling to body composition, key wellness indicators, and movement analysis. For the world's largest organisations, that means the data to better equip, protect, and care for their people. Immediately, and over time.

Three large markets. One platform.

Bodd operates across three sectors — each with significant scale, clear value proposition, and strong commercial traction.

Uniforms & Workforce

Defence forces, airlines, emergency services, and uniformed workforces globally — replacing manual fitting with rapid, accurate, self-serve body scanning at any scale.

Retail & Apparel

High-function apparel brands where fit has a correct answer — tailoring, made-to-measure, and sporting apparel — delivering precise sizing intelligence at scale.

Health & Wellness

Pharmacies, gyms, longevity centres, corporate wellness programmes, and allied health providers — building the body data infrastructure for better health outcomes.

Why now.

Three converging trends are creating an extraordinary opportunity for a platform like Bodd.

The data economy is expanding into the physical world

Organisations are increasingly making decisions based on data — and body data is one of the last major data categories to be properly captured and used at scale.

Workforce health is a boardroom issue

From GLP-1 programmes to duty-of-care obligations, organisations are investing in the physical health of their people like never before — and they need data to do it well.

The hardware moment has arrived

Precision manufacturing, advances in sensing technology, and the maturation of enterprise cloud infrastructure have made a platform like Bodd possible — and defensible — for the first time.
